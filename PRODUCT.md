# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Adults in the Spaichingen / Tuttlingen / Trossingen region dealing with chronic or acute physical complaints: tension, trigger points, fascial restrictions, post-sport soreness, or desk-job strain. They arrive via Google search or word of mouth. Their context: they have real pain and are weighing whether to trust a therapist they haven't met. They need to feel convinced quickly — not sold to, but recognized.

Secondary audience: fitness professionals and health practitioners who consider Wadim's online certification courses.

## Product Purpose

Faszienpraxis Fech (brand: Fech Puls) is a solo fascia therapy practice run by Wadim Fech in Spaichingen, Germany. The site's job is to convert a pained person's search into a booked appointment. Success looks like: visitor lands, trusts, and books — ideally on the first visit.

The site also surfaces online certification courses as a secondary revenue stream.

## Brand Personality

Premium. Editorial. Authoritative.

Voice: direct, precise, unhurried. Speaks like a skilled craftsperson who knows exactly what they're doing — not a salesperson. No fluff. No wellness platitudes. Every sentence earns its place.

Emotional goal: confidence. The patient should leave the landing page thinking "this person knows what they're doing" — not "what a nice website."

## Anti-references

- Generic wellness/spa aesthetic: stock photo leaves, soft gradients, lotus flowers, "holistic healing" language
- Cold clinical / hospital white: sterile grids, no warmth, feels like a waiting room
- SaaS landing page templates: hero metric boxes, gradient text, identical feature card grids
- Dark luxury / nightclub aesthetic: heavy blacks, gold foil, unapproachable opacity
- Generic AI output: anything that looks like it could have been built for any business in any category

## Design Principles

1. **Precision over warmth.** Show craft through restraint and exactness, not stock imagery or reassuring color palettes.
2. **The body as the subject.** Every design decision should serve readability and direct communication — not decoration.
3. **Editorial confidence.** Typography, spacing, and hierarchy should feel like a considered publication, not a template.
4. **Trust through texture.** Social proof, credentials, and specificity (names, numbers, locations) are design elements, not afterthoughts.
5. **One action, always visible.** The booking CTA is the north star. Every section should make it feel closer, not optional.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

General adult audience — no specific WCAG level mandated. Maintain sufficient contrast, keyboard navigability, and screen reader semantics as baseline. Reduced-motion support already in place.
